Nutritional Focus
High-Fiber Foods: Help improve satiety and control hunger.
Vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes.
Lean Proteins: Preserve muscle and keep you full.
Chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, lentils, beans, Greek yogurt.
Healthy Fats: Essential for health and energy.
Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish (if non-vegetarian).
Avoid:
Sugary drinks and processed snacks.
Refined grains (white bread, pastries).
High-fat fried foods.

 Additional Recommendations
Portion Control: Use smaller plates and bowls.
Hydration: Drink 2–3 liters of water daily.
Physical Activity: Include 150–300 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise and strength training 2–3 times/week.
Sleep: Ensure 7–9 hours of quality sleep per night to support metabolism.
Behavioral Changes:
Keep a food journal to track intake.
Avoid eating in front of screens.
Practice mindful eating by chewing slowly and recognizing hunger cues.


For Veg Only:

Food Choices
Protein-Rich Foods:

Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans.
Tofu, tempeh, seitan, edamame.
Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, low-fat paneer.
Plant-based protein powders (e.g., pea, rice, soy protein).
Healthy Carbohydrates:

Whole grains: Brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole-grain bread, and pasta.
Starchy vegetables: Sweet potatoes, squash.
Legumes and lentils (double as protein sources).
Healthy Fats:

Nuts: Almonds, walnuts, cashews (in moderation).
Seeds: Chia, flax, pumpkin, and sunflower.
Avocado and olive oil.
Vegetables and Fruits:

Non-starchy vegetables: Broccoli, spinach, kale, zucchini, bell peppers, mushrooms.
Fruits: Apples, oranges, berries, pears (lower sugar options).

Behavioral Tips
Meal Timing:

Aim for 3 main meals and 1–2 small snacks daily to avoid overeating.
Portion Control:

Use smaller plates and mindful eating techniques to recognize satiety.
Hydration:

Drink at least 2–3 liters of water daily.
Exercise:

Combine moderate aerobic activity (walking, swimming, cycling) with strength training to preserve muscle mass.
Supplementation:

Consider a vitamin B12 supplement if needed (common in vegetarians).
Include fortified foods or supplements for iron, zinc, and omega-3s (from algae oil).
